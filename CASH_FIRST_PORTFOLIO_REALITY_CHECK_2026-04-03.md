# Cash-First Portfolio Reality Check

Date: 2026-04-03

## Locked Context

- Goal: maximize the probability of collecting at least `EUR 1,000` by `May 3, 2026`
- New spend cap this month: `EUR 50`
- Market bias: Portugal first
- Sales motion allowed: in-person, outbound, cold-walk, calls, DMs
- Reality standard: cash collected matters more than MRR or product elegance
- Important founder edge: years in restaurants, comfortable with direct presence and sales

## Executive Answer

If the only goal is "what gets you to cash fastest from this exact portfolio," the safest answer is not a consumer AI app and not a polished SaaS build. It is a service-first local offer.

My ranking is:

1. `Web Agency`, but narrowed to a restaurant-first or local-SMB website sprint with strong upfront deposits
2. `Table IA`, but only if you stop treating it like a `EUR 29/mo` SaaS and sell it as a paid pilot/setup offer first
3. `Momentum AI`, only if you decide to go back to remote B2B selling and accept a slower first-cash window

The most honest split is:

- Fastest path to `EUR 1,000` cash: `Web Agency`
- Best differentiated restaurant wedge worth preserving: `Table IA`
- Best longer-game B2B service thesis: `Momentum AI` / `Sigil AI`

## Method

Scores below are built for this month, not for abstract startup upside.

Weights:

- `30-day cash speed` - 20%
- `ease of closing without audience` - 15%
- `fit with your real edge and sales style` - 15%
- `build scope inside 30 days` - 15%
- `cash cost fit inside EUR 50` - 10%
- `Portugal buyer willingness to pay` - 10%
- `delivery burden after first sale` - 5%
- `legal / trust / compliance friction` - 5%
- `repeatability and month-2 leverage` - 5%

Raw metric scores are `1-10`. Weighted score is shown as `/100`.

Important assumption:

- when a project is only viable this month as a service-first offer, I scored the service-first packaging, not the most optimistic SaaS version in the docs

## Part 2 - Top Contenders Deep Dive

### 1. `Web Agency` - the safest cash machine

Why it ranks first:

- It already has what matters for month-1 cash: sales scripts, contracts, proposals, intake forms, niche prompts, and a clear SOP.
- The offer is understandable in 10 seconds. A business owner already knows what a website is and what a bad one costs them.
- Portugal pricing is not amazing, but it is still compatible with `EUR 1,000` cash if you collect strong upfront deposits.
- You can sell this without pretending it is SaaS.

Why it is still not perfect:

- It is crowded.
- It is harder to build a moat.
- If you drift into "I build websites for anyone," you will become another freelancer fighting on price.

The winning version:

- narrow it to restaurants and nearby local SMBs
- show one niche-specific demo before asking for money
- collect a large kickoff deposit
- use the same lead list later to upsell `Table IA`

Reality check:

- This is the least glamorous answer in the top tier
- it is also the one most likely to put real cash in your account before May 3

### 2. `Table IA` - the best restaurant wedge, but the current pricing is wrong

Why it still ranks high:

- best founder-market fit in the portfolio for this month
- highly demo-able in person
- restaurant owners can understand it instantly when the demo is about their own menu, wine, kitchen hours, and languages
- far more differentiated than generic website work

Why it does not rank first:

- the repo's current `EUR 29/mo` model is a month-1 cash trap
- at `EUR 29/mo`, you would need roughly `35` paying restaurants to collect `EUR 1,000` in 30 days
- that is not a serious emergency-cash plan
- there is real trust friction around wrong answers, allergens, waitstaff process, and owner skepticism

The only version that works this month:

- charge a pilot or setup fee first
- keep the subscription secondary
- scope the product tightly around recommendations, FAQs, and multilingual table assistance
- do not sell "AI waiter" or anything that sounds operationally risky

My honest view:

- `Table IA` is a stronger wedge than most of the portfolio
- but it becomes a real business this month only if you sell it like a service-first pilot, not like a cheap SaaS

### 3. `Momentum AI` - good business, wrong month

Why it is still top four:

- the docs describe a credible service-first GTM engine
- the offer can command meaningful ACV when sold well
- it maps to premium B2B pain, not consumer wishful thinking

Why it is not a month-1 winner:

- the local Portugal-first constraint hurts it
- the recommended `zero-risk POC` slows immediate cash collection
- it is remote B2B selling, not high-velocity local closing
- there is no evidence you currently have warm GTM buyers ready to shorten the cycle

Reality check:

- this is a better "next serious company" candidate than a "need cash in 30 days" candidate

### 4. `Renda Facil - Contract generation` - real product, slow trust cycle

Why it deserves respect:

- real deployed product
- real pricing
- strong regulatory pain and real market need

Why it is not your month-1 answer:

- too much trust burden for cold acquisition
- legal/compliance questions extend the sales cycle
- existing pricing is too low for emergency cash unless volume is already there
- agencies and legal professionals can be valuable, but they do not usually buy instantly from a cold approach

Reality check:

- this could become a solid niche SaaS
- it is not the best way to solve your cash problem right now

## Part 4 - Brutal Truths

### The portfolio-wide truths

1. Most of the portfolio is optimized for "interesting" or "high-upside," not "cash in 30 days."
2. The biggest hidden enemy is not product quality. It is distribution speed.
3. Consumer subscriptions are almost all fake comfort under your current constraints. The price points are too low and the audience is not already there.
4. Several strong ideas become weak month-1 businesses because the current pricing is too cheap for your cash goal.

### The specific truths that matter most

- `Table IA` at `EUR 29/mo` is not a real emergency-cash model.
- `Renda Facil` is real, but trust-heavy and underpriced for emergency cash unless you already have a warm channel.
- `Momentum AI` and `Sigil AI` are better when you are optimizing for ACV and long-term positioning, not local Portugal cash velocity.
- `FlowForge - Freestyle` is already monetized, but its own docs say meaningful paid growth wants far more than your current budget.
- `Angel AI` and `Mind Map AI` are emotionally compelling, but they are dangerous distractions right now.
- `Studio-Mix` is the kind of thing you build when cash pressure is lower, not higher.
- `Chimera Studio` is still too dependent on content volume and distribution that you do not currently control.

### The one pricing truth you should not ignore

The fastest route to `EUR 1,000` this month is not "find 100 small subscribers."

It is:

- collect `2-3` serious upfront payments
- from buyers who already understand the problem
- using an offer they can say yes to quickly

That is why service-first local sales win this month.

## Part 5 - Final Recommendation Stack

### Primary bet

`Web Agency`, narrowed to a restaurant-first or local-SMB website sprint.

Why:

- highest probability of collecting `EUR 1,000` before May 3
- already has operational assets in the repo
- sells cleanly with deposits
- uses your outbound ability immediately
- lets you reuse the same lead pool later for `Table IA`

### Fallback bet if the first stalls within 7-10 days

`Table IA`, but only as a paid pilot/setup offer.

Use it if:

- restaurant owners react strongly to demos
- your personal presence gives it more energy than a generic website pitch
- you can get fast verbal interest from multiple restaurants in the first week

Do not use it as:

- a cheap subscription-only SaaS
- a complex voice-tech build
- an allergen or operational guarantee product

## My Most Honest Recommendation

If you want the most rational answer, it is this:

- sell `Web Agency` now
- use restaurants as the niche
- use `Table IA` as the differentiator and second-layer upsell
- do not spend April trying to force a consumer app or a premium B2B system to solve a local cash emergency

If you insist on betting on the most emotionally alive idea in the set, `Table IA` is the one I would allow. But only with a hard rule:

- if you do not get at least `2` serious restaurant follow-ups or `1` paid pilot inside the first `7-10` days, stop and switch fully to the website offer

That is the most cash-honest way to preserve upside without gambling the month.
